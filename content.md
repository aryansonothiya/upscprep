## 📝 The Hindu Editorial Analysis
*   **Topic:** The RBI and its Growing Fiscal Role 🏦
*   **Context:** The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) recently approved a **record surplus transfer of ₹2.87 lakh crore to the Union government for FY26**, igniting discussions about its evolving role and fiscal significance beyond traditional monetary stability.
*   **Key Takeaways:**
    *   **Fiscal Significance:** This unprecedented transfer highlights RBI's increasing contribution to government finances, prompting questions about central bank independence and the intersection of monetary and fiscal policies.
    *   **Economic Capital Framework:** While consistent with the revised Economic Capital Framework, the sheer scale of the transfer (exceeding annual budgets of several Indian States) suggests the RBI is increasingly functioning as a fiscal instrument.
    *   **Reserve Management:** A significant portion of the surplus stems from gains from foreign assets, foreign exchange transactions, and interest earned on securities holdings, underscoring how RBI's reserve management influences fiscal revenues.
    *   **Fiscal Federalism Concern:** As this non-tax revenue directly benefits the Union government and is excluded from the divisible pool shared with States, it implicitly supports **fiscal centralisation**, raising questions about the federal balance.
    *   **Institutional Distance:** While the RBI maintains operational autonomy within a defined framework, the growing fiscal importance of its earnings could challenge the preservation of institutional distance from government fiscal compulsions.

## 🏛️ Polity & Governance

*   **Main Points:**
    *   **Legislative Development:** Madhya Pradesh Chief Minister Mohan Yadav announced that a **Bill on the Uniform Civil Code (UCC) would be introduced in the upcoming Monsoon Session of the Legislative Assembly**, scheduled from July 20-24.
    *   **Drafting Committee:** A six-member high-level committee, constituted by the State government in April and chaired by retired Supreme Court judge Justice Ranjana Prasad Desai, is currently working on the draft Bill.

    *   **Significance:** If enacted, MP would follow other BJP-ruled States like Uttarakhand and Gujarat in implementing the UCC, marking a significant step towards fulfilling a long-standing party agenda.

## 🌍 Geography & Environment
*   **Current Issue:** Bangladesh's New Padma Barrage 🌊
*   **Main Points:**
    *   **Project Overview:** Bangladesh has greenlit the new **Padma barrage**, a 2.1-km-long structure designed to control the Padma river (known as Ganga in Bangladesh) and alleviate the country's seasonal water scarcity. It aims to store 2,900 million cubic meters of water at an estimated cost of ₹39,170 crore.
    *   **Location & Context:** Positioned just 180 km downstream of India's Farakka barrage, which Bangladesh has historically linked to periodic water scarcity and reduced water flow, impacting the 1996 Ganges Water Treaty.
    *   **Environmental Concerns:** Experts warn of substantial environmental impacts, including reduced groundwater recharge, increased salinity in the Sundarbans (harming brackish water ecosystems), silt accumulation leading to floods, and adverse effects on local fisherfolk livelihoods.
    *   **Strategic Implications:** While offering Bangladesh more control over its water resources, the project (potentially supported by China) could heighten India's discomfort, particularly amidst existing river-sharing disputes like the Teesta.
